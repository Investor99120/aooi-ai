# Claim Rules

## Purpose

Aooi must protect brands from unsafe, exaggerated, medicalised, or unverifiable claims.

This is especially important for FriendRedLight because red light therapy content can easily cross into healthcare or treatment language.

## Global Rules

- Prefer conservative wellness language.
- Avoid healthcare advice unless reviewed by qualified professionals and approved by the operator.
- Do not invent scientific certainty.
- Do not promise outcomes.
- Do not use fake reviews or fake social proof.
- Do not publish product specifications unless verified.

## Approved Language Pattern

Use: gentle, supports, designed for, can be part of, helps create, routine, wellness, comfort, relaxation, consistency, self-care, home wellness, evening routine, recovery routine.

Example:

> FriendRedLight is designed for calm home wellness routines and can be part of a consistent evening self-care ritual.

## Blocked Language Pattern

Avoid: cure, treat, heal disease, guaranteed pain relief, anti-inflammatory cure, sleep disorder treatment, disease prevention, clinical result promises, and doctor-approved cure.

## Rewrite Pattern

Unsafe:

> Red light treats insomnia.

Safer:

> Red light can be part of a calm pre-bed routine, but FriendRedLight does not present this as sleep-disorder care or a promised sleep outcome.

## Brand-Level Files

Each brand can extend these rules in `brands/{brand}/claim_whitelist.md` and `brands/{brand}/claim_blacklist.md`.
